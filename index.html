Introduction

The rapid iteration and proliferation of advances in the field of deep learning and artificial intelligence have led the world into a new era of data driven decision making which will likely only gain traction as algorithms become more robust and advanced. Along with the powerful forward momentum of machine learning research, there have also been numerous subfields which are developing at an ever increasing pace. Supervised learning has spawned self-supervised, semi-supervised, and unsupervised variants while meta learning has arisen to fill the need of quick adaptations from environments with limited context. In addition to theoretical research, there have been extraordinary advances in the ability to deploy production models with billions of parameters distributed across numerous nodes which has given new persepctive to the possible depths of deep learning.

Our workshop aims to bring together top researchers working on cutting edge advances across every subfield of the machine learning community. We believe that every opportunity to meet (online or in person) to discuss relevant advances, engage in open discussion, and receive critical feedback is an invaluable resource to the community. We welcome and invite all researchers to be a part of our workshop.

Call for Submissions

We invite submissions for the First KAIST Machine Learning Workshop that brings together researchers in machine learning and their applications.

Topics of interest include (but are not limited to):

  1. General Machine Learning (active learning, clustering, online learning, ranking, reinforcement learning, semi-supervised learning, time series analysis, unsupervised learning, etc.)
  2. Deep Learning (architectures, generative models, deep reinforcement learning, etc.)
  3. Learning Theory (bandits, game theory, statistical learning theory, etc.)
  4. Optimization (convex and non-convex optimization, matrix/tensor methods, sparsity, etc.)
  5. Probabilistic Inference (Bayesian methods, graphical models, Monte Carlo methods, etc.)
  6. Trustworthy Machine Learning (accountability, causality, fairness, privacy, robustness, safety etc.)
  7. Applications (computational biology, audio and speech processing, natural language processing, healthcare, neuroscience, social good, climate science, etc.)

The papers should be 6-8 pages in ICLR 2020 format excluding references.

Submissions should be made to Open Review
